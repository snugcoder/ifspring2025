Bubble tea, lovingly known as boba, is more than just a drink—it's a global phenomenon. Originating in Taiwan in the 1980s, it has since captured hearts worldwide with its perfect blend of tea, milk, and chewy tapioca pearls. From cozy neighborhood cafés to international chains like Gong Cha, Sharetea, Tiger Sugar, and Kung Fu Tea, boba has become a symbol of culture, creativity, and community. This microsite is inspired by the Wikipedia page on Bubble Tea, which serves as a detailed, ever-growing archive of its history, evolution, and impact. Here's to the drink that continues to bring joy—one sip and one tapioca pearl at a time.
